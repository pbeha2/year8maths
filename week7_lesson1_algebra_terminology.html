Learning intention
We are learning to identify and use the language of algebra.
By the end of this lesson you will be able to:
  • identify the pronumeral, coefficient, constant term, and number of terms in an algebraic expression
  • explain what a pronumeral represents in a real-world situation
  • write a simple algebraic expression from a written description
0 – 5 min The hook
JK
Jordan Kim
Content creator · Gaming and lifestyle
84K
Followers
$3
Per 1K views
$150
Per sponsorship

Jordan is a Year 8 student who started posting gaming videos last year. The channel has grown to 84,000 followers. Jordan earns $3 for every 1,000 views and $150 for each sponsored post.

Jordan wants to know: how much could the channel earn this month? The problem is — views and sponsorships change every month. They are not fixed numbers. They are unknowns.

This is exactly why algebra exists. When a value is unknown or changes, we use a letter to represent it. Those letters have names — and so do all the other parts of an algebraic expression. Today you learn those names.

5 – 20 min Terminology — the four building blocks
Pronumeral
A letter that stands in for a number that is unknown or can change. It is the variable.
v = view-thousands
Coefficient
The number multiplied by a pronumeral. It tells you the rate — how much you earn per unit.
3 in 3v means $3 per 1K views
Constant term
A fixed number in an expression. It does not change, no matter what the pronumerals equal.
50 — a fixed monthly bonus
Term
A single part of an expression — a number, a pronumeral, or both multiplied together.
3v is one term · 50 is one term
Jordan's monthly earnings expression — labelled
3v + 150s + 50
Pronumerals
v and s
Coefficients
3 and 150
Constant
50
No. of terms
3
  • v
    v = view-thousands — it changes each month, so it is a pronumeral
  • s
    s = number of sponsorships — also unknown, also a pronumeral
  • 3
    3 is the coefficient of v — Jordan earns $3 per view-thousand, so v is multiplied by 3
  • 150
    150 is the coefficient of s — Jordan earns $150 per sponsorship
  • 50
    50 is the constant — a fixed monthly platform bonus that never changes
20 – 35 min Guided practice — work through these with your teacher
Questions 1 – 3
Q1
A rival creator Alex earns money differently. Their expression is:
5v + 200s
Write down: the pronumerals, the coefficients, and the number of terms.
Pronumerals: v and s  ·  Coefficients: 5 and 200  ·  Number of terms: 2  ·  No constant term — Alex has no fixed monthly income, only variable earnings. Notice the expression has fewer terms than Jordan's.
Q2
Jordan adds merchandise sales. The new expression is:
3v + 150s + 12m + 50
What does m represent? What is its coefficient? How many terms does the expression have now?
m represents the number of merchandise items sold. Coefficient of m: 12 — Jordan earns $12 per item sold. Number of terms: 4 — three variable terms plus the constant 50.
Q3
Which expression has a higher earning rate per view-thousand — Jordan's 3v + 150s + 50 or Alex's 5v + 200s? How can you tell just by looking at the expression, without substituting any values?
Alex earns more per view-thousand. The coefficient of v in Alex's expression is 5, compared to Jordan's 3. The coefficient tells you the rate directly — you don't need to know the actual number of views. This is one reason algebraic expressions are useful: you can compare rates at a glance.
35 – 50 min Independent practice — try these on your own
Questions 4 – 6 — increasing challenge
Q4
Identify the coefficient of each pronumeral, the constant term, and the number of terms in this expression:
7x + 3y − 4
Pronumerals: x and y  ·  Coefficient of x: 7  ·  Coefficient of y: 3  ·  Constant term: −4  ·  Number of terms: 3. Note: the constant term is negative. Constants can be positive or negative.
Q5
A gaming streamer earns $8 for every 1,000 views and $250 per sponsorship. They also pay $30 per month for streaming software.
Write an algebraic expression for their monthly profit. Define your pronumerals clearly before you write the expression.
Let v = view-thousands, s = number of sponsorships

Expression: 8v + 250s − 30

The −30 is a constant term representing the fixed monthly software cost. Because it is a cost and not income, it is subtracted. This is your first example of algebra modelling a real financial situation — income minus expenses.
Q6 — Think deeper
Two creators compare their monthly earnings expressions:

Creator A: 4v + 100s + 80     Creator B: 6v + 80s
Creator A says they will always earn more because they have three terms. Creator B disagrees. Who is right? Explain your thinking — you do not need to substitute values to give a strong answer.
Neither is always right — it depends on the values of v and s.

If views are very high, Creator B earns more per view-thousand (coefficient 6 vs 4). If sponsorships are high, Creator A earns more per sponsorship (coefficient 100 vs 80) and also has the $80 constant income. Having more terms does not mean a higher value — it depends on what the pronumerals equal. This is the key insight: expressions describe relationships, not fixed amounts.

3 questions · 3 minutes · not graded
1. In the expression 9p + 4q − 15, write down the coefficient of q and the constant term.
2. A food delivery driver earns $6 per delivery and receives a $20 daily bonus. Write an algebraic expression for their daily earnings. Define your pronumeral.
3. In one sentence: explain what a pronumeral is and why it is useful.
